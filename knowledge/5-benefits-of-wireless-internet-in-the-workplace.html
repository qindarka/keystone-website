5 Benefits of Wireless Internet in the Workplace

May 11, 2018 · John Woods

Whether your office or business uses a mix of wired and wireless connections or relies solely on wireless, there are many benefits wireless internet offers. It creates flexibility, reduces costs and can be a value-added service for customers, clients, business partners and vendors.

If you still haven’t implemented wireless internet, here are four reasons you may want to consider it: 

1: Access the network from multiple devices

As workplaces are evolving with technology, more devices are being leveraged for day-to-day tasks. Phones and tablets are proving to be useful in meetings, brainstorm sessions and presentations. Overall, wireless is a great option when multiple screens and devices are required at once since it ensures these devices can be seamlessly integrated into daily tasks.

2: Foster collaboration

The ability to use multiple devices in an office, in turn, improves collaboration among employees. It offers flexibility to work at different workstations, in conference rooms, coffee shops and other collaborative spaces. Wireless internet keeps employees connected to emails, files and shared drives while they benefit from mobility around the office. As a result, working groups can edit documents collaboratively, which helps increase creativity and productivity.

3: Flexibility of workspace

As companies develop, it’s likely workstations will have to shift in order to accommodate growing teams. A wireless internet connection can help streamline these transitions.

Many companies are moving towards unassigned workstations and laptops are quickly replacing traditional desktop workstations. A wireless connection is essential for these types of emerging trends in the workplace.

4: Cost-effect network expansion

Wireless internet is a great solution for growing companies since new employees can be added to wireless networks without the costly installation of cables and wires. Overall, wireless is a quick and cost-effective way to add new users to your network

5: Value-added service for your customers

If you’re a service-based business, wireless internet can give you a competitive edge and increase customer satisfaction. It’s also a great option to offer vendors and suppliers when they visit your office, and allows them to stay connected to their work during breaks.
