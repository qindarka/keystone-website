Windows 10 End of Life – What You Need to Know

July 9, 2025 · Will Smith

Earlier this year, Microsoft has announced that Windows 10 will reach its end of support on October 14, 2025. After this date, Windows 10 will no longer receive critical security updates, bug fixes, or technical support from Microsoft. This means that any vulnerabilities discovered after October 2025 will remain unpatched, putting your business at greater risk of cyberattacks, malware, and data breaches.

Why does this Matter?

There are plenty of reasons why Windows 10 reaching end of life is important, including:

  • 1. Increased Security Risks: Without regular security updates, Windows 10 devices become prime targets for cyber attacks.
  • 2. Compliance Concerns: Many industry regulations require the use of supported software. Continuing to use Windows 10 after its end of life could put your business at risk of non-compliance.
  • 3. Limited Support: Microsoft will no longer provide technical assistance or troubleshooting for Windows 10 issues after this date.
  • 4. Compatibility Issues: Over time, new software and services may no longer be compatible with Windows 10, limiting your business capabilities.

What can be Done?

To keep your business secure and operational, we strongly recommend upgrading to new computers that support Windows 11. Upgrading now will help you avoid security risks, ensure compliance, and provide your team with the latest productivity tools.

Some devices may already be compatible with Windows 11, so they can be updated from Windows 10 without the need to pick up a new device. Here’s how you can check if your device is Windows 11 Compatible:

  • 1. Ensure your device meets the minimum system requirements
  • 2. Download the PC Health Check tool from Microsoft’s official Windows 11 page.
  • 3. Install and Open the PC Health Check Tool
  • 4. Click the “Check Now” button. This will scan your system, and check for Windows 11 Compatibility.
  • 5. Upon completion, the tool will tell you if your laptop meets the Windows 11 requirements and specify any missing requirements.
